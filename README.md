# Cyclistic Bike-Share: Optimizing Digital Marketing Conversion via Behavioral Data

**[👉 Click Here to View the Live Looker Studio Dashboard](https://datastudio.google.com/reporting/23ce92eb-6258-4112-ae02-a0a3020c93b8)**

## 1. Executive Summary
Directed a data-driven marketing strategy to maximize high-margin annual memberships by analyzing over 5.6 million historical bike-share trips. Engineered a robust SQL data pipeline in Google BigQuery to consolidate, clean, and transform 12 months of unaggregated system data, bypassing the physical limitations of traditional spreadsheet software. By developing an interactive Looker Studio dashboard, I visualized distinct temporal cohorts—contrasting high-efficiency weekday commuters with extended-duration weekend explorers. These insights equipped the executive team with the exact behavioral targets and seasonal timelines required to launch a high-ROI digital conversion campaign.

## 2. The Business Task
To analyze historical Cyclistic bike-share trip data to identify, quantify, and map the behavioral and operational differences between casual riders and annual members. These data-driven insights form the foundation of a targeted digital marketing strategy aimed at converting existing casual riders into highly profitable annual members.

## 3. Data Sourcing & Integrity (Prepare)
* **Data Origin:** 12 months of primary, geotracked operational data (May 2025 to April 2026) generated by Motivate International Inc. 
* **Volume:** 12 independent CSV files merged into a single dataset of 5,697,455 rows.
* **Privacy & Security:** The dataset is strictly anonymized, stripping all Personally Identifiable Information (PII) to comply with data protection standards.
* **Integrity Verification:** Initial validation confirmed zero duplicate primary keys (`ride_id`) upon ingestion.

## 4. Data Cleaning & Transformation Pipeline (Process)
Due to the massive scale of the dataset (5.7M+ rows), traditional spreadsheet tools were bypassed. Data consolidation and cleaning were executed programmatically using **Google BigQuery (SQL)**.

**Anomalies Mitigated:**
* Filtered out 29 temporal anomalies (where end times occurred before start times).
* Removed short-duration noise (trips under 60 seconds) to eliminate hardware-related false starts.

**Feature Engineering:**
* Extracted specific temporal elements: `hour_of_day`, `day_of_week`, and `month_name`.
* Engineered a custom `ride_duration_bucket` column to categorically segment user behaviors (e.g., '0-10 Mins', '1+ Hours').

*(The full, reproducible SQL cleaning script is available in this repository as `combined_tripdata_cleaned.sql`)*

## 5. Analysis & Insights (Analyze & Share)
Based on the aggregated metrics, distinct behavioral patterns between Casual Riders and Annual Members emerged across three core dimensions:

* **The Commuter vs. The Explorer (Weekly Trends):** Annual Members peak during the traditional workweek (Tuesday–Thursday), with rigidly consistent ride durations (~11–13 minutes), indicating routine transit. Casual Riders skew heavily toward the weekend. Saturday is their highest volume day (399,124 rides), and they spend nearly twice as much time on the bikes (averaging 25.72 minutes), indicating leisure and recreation.
* **Seasonal Elasticity:** Casual riders are highly sensitive to seasonality. Their volume experiences a rapid, aggressive spike at the onset of summer, jumping from ~176k rides in May to over 324k by August, before plummeting by over 90% in the winter.

## 6. Strategic Recommendations (Act)
To maximize the conversion of casual riders into high-margin annual memberships, Cyclistic should implement:

1. **The "Summer Kickoff" Campaign:** Allocate the majority of the digital marketing budget between late April and July to capitalize on the massive seasonal momentum of casual riders.
2. **Weekend-Targeted Digital Interventions:** Leverage app notifications or email follow-ups deployed specifically on weekend afternoons, highlighting how much money a casual rider would have saved on their extended 30+ minute ride if they were a member.
3. **Introduce a "Weekend Explorer" Membership Tier:** Bridge the gap for leisure riders by offering a lower-cost "Weekend-Only" annual pass to lock them into the subscription ecosystem.
